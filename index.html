What Is An Animation Studio?

An Animation Studio is a place where major animated shows are mainly created. These studios sizes can vary from 100-2,000 active employees. These studios follow a specific pipeline in order produce high quality works year round. Each person has a hand full of roles to fufill

Different studios specialize in their own creative way of expressing themselves, some studios like Studio Trigger have experience with using real life props (i.e Panty and Stocking with Gartabelt), while other animation studios like Studio Orange or Studio Mappa specialize integrating 3D works into their shows or handle 3D animation overall.

Who Is Involved?

As said before, Animation Studios tend to have around 100-2,000 workers each with their own role in the pipeline. The most common role in these productions are the Animators and Directors. Roles like these go even further and have sub catagories or sub-roles that handle their own separate tasks



For instance, the average production there is usually a Series Director, Assistant Director, Animation Director and Chief Animation Director. Most of the time people can undergo more than 3-4 of these roles.

Yoh Yoshinari: Studio Trigger Behind the Scenes 2013

Additional Information

Each role in the Animation Pipeline is important, with each worker using the previous persons drawings as a way to further the pipeline.

Anime Pipelines specifically follow this order:

Pre Production and Planning, Storyboarding, LO/Rough Animation, (Chief) Animation Director Corrections, Genga/Clean-up work, Additional Cleanup and Inbetweening, Coloring/Paint, and Compositing/Editing
